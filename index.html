Cappuccino

Cappuccino (Italian: Cappuccino) is a type of coffee drink based on sweetened coffee without espresso, which is prepared with steamed milk foam (microfoam). Types of drinks include using cream instead of milk, using non-dairy milk substitutes, and sometimes using cinnamon or chocolate powder.

espresso

Espresso (in Italian: Espresso) is a type of thick coffee that was invented in Turin, Italy in the early 20th century. Espresso is made by passing water close to the boiling point with high pressure through the coffee powder. Espresso is a coffee with a higher concentration of dissolved and suspended solids. Espresso is mostly extracted by an espresso machine.
